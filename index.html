HOW I DESIGNED THIS COURSE


By Andrew Dalton Updated August 24, 2022




Coding can be excessively difficult and extremely hard to understand for your first time. In fact, a lot of hardworking people step up to the plate without success. Not just in programming but different career as well. They try their hearts out but end of failing.

This most likely happens because the more they start doing something, the harder it becomes and more stress life brings on to them. Life is complicated and it's also limited. This makes a person panic and end up doing what is more comfortable for them. They start thinking, 'what will I get out of this' or 'why am I not good enough'.This is a very visious cycle, and I'm here to try and help make life a little bit less complicated.

I found the best programming sources online, so you don't have to stress about finding every little detail because their's a lot of crap that goes into programming. The first time a beginner or someone tries to code they may feel lost, or hopeless.

The best way to solve these problems is to think for a moment, 'do I really want this' or 'is it worth my time'. I occasionally ask my self these things, and it really helps me cut out the distractions. If we start focusing on this, it will help bring our dreams into light.

We humans work better coding in a group, or in a fixed section. Most people fall off when they have to learn all these things in an unfixed manner because we want to stay in our comfort zone.

I hope these tutorials really help you stay in a fixed motion, and try not to focus to much on distractions around you. It may take a lot of time to try to find the best websites, the best videos, or the best courses online. This is the reason I created this, so I could save your time. I hope these will help you. KEEP CODING...
